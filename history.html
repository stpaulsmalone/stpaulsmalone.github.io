History

Timeline

1837 White settlement begins in Calumet Township
1846 October 6: Parcel of land deeded to trustees of the “German Lutheran Congregation”  (including John Thuerwachter, Sr.) for church use. The community probably met in members’ homes and were served by “circuit riders” before this.
St. Paul's is the first Protestant church in what comes to be known as the "Holyland" by nearly twenty years.
  Log Cabin church built on cemetery property burns before completion
1847 Second log church begun; completed in 1848. It is believed to have stood near the corner of Highway HH and St. Paul Rd.
1849 N. Wolkert, first recorded pastor resigns over controversy about his “exclusive attitude.”
1850 Motion made to unite with church in New Holstein
  Church renamed “United Evangelical Lutheran Congregation”
Until 1870, most ministers appear to be drawn from the Wisconsin Lutheran Synod.
1858 First reference to “Evangelical Lutheran St. Paul’s Church”
1866 Parsonage constructed; pastor teaches in log cabin school "near the church" 3 days a week
1870 Arrival of Rev. Rahmeier of the Evangelical Synod, a predecessor of
the United Church of Christ. After this point, ministers seem to be exclusively from the Evangelical Synod and successor bodies.
1873 Schoolhouse built
1892 Present church built at cost of $5000 (~$135,000 today)
1897 Stable built for pastor’s horses
Baptismal font added to sanctuary
1912 New parsonage built
1918 Use of German in worship ends
Women's Guild organized, though it is suspected a women's group existed previously
1922 John Hille ordained at St. Paul's
1926 Church and parsonage receive electricity; parsonage gets running water
Rev. Paul Hoeppner dies at age 62 while serving the congregation. He is the first of several pastors to be buried in the St. Paul's cemetery.
1927 Phone line installed in parsonage
A.H. Grauer ordained to serve congregation
1930 Rudolph Kalwitz ordained at St. Paul's
1944-1945 Sanctuary refurbished
“Christ in Gethsemane” altar painting by Rev. E.F. Wilking installed
1946 100th anniversary of congregation. Rev. F.O. Claussen insists in centennial history that St. Paul's "stands today on a Lutheran foundation as much as it ever did," citing 1918 church constitution accepting Augsburg Confession, Luther's Catechism and Heidelberg Catechism.
1947 Steeple struck by lightning; rebuilt tower shorter than original
1955 After seven years of discussion, basement added to sanctuary building
1957 Formation of the United Church of Christ. St. Paul's will be incorporated into the new denomination in 1961 with the founding of the Wisconsin Conference.
1965 500 Norway Spruce and 500 White Cedar trees planted in cemetery
1968 Parsonage expanded with office, bedroom, utility room and rear entrance
1976 St. Paul’s yokes with Stockbridge Methodist Church
1984 Property by pond (current location of Stone Soup Trail) purchased.
450 Spruce trees planted on new property, but due to drought, only about 3 survive.
1987 "Vesper Point" added to south end of pond property
1998 Yoke dissolved as Stockbridge Methodist Church closes
1999 Keith Weiland called, with idea that he will close St. Paul’s. It doesn't work.
2015 Holyland Food Pantry founded in ecumenical partnership. The pantry is located in St. Paul's parsonage.
2016 Rev. Daniel Schultz, 45th pastor, called
