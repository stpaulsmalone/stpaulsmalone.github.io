About St. Paul's


You're welcome here. No, seriously: no excuses, no exceptions.

A few things to know about us:

  • Our pastor since 2016 is Rev. Daniel Schultz.
  • We are part of the Wisconsin Conference of the United Church of Christ.
  • We're a family-sized church that prides itself on close relationships and supportive community.
  • We are open, accepting, and welcoming. We don't discriminate, we don't judge, and we don't hold anyone back from the communion table. We know we've got a good thing going, and we want to share it with the world.
  • We are an intentionally intergenerational learning community.
  • We live in a scenic bend in the road and support a nature trail. See for yourself!
  • Our generations go back. Way back. All the way back to 1846.
  • We are committed to ministry to the world, in our local food pantry and around the world.

Worship at St. Paul's

Worship services are held at 10:00 a.m. Sunday morning. Watch a few sermons to get a feel for what we're like, or read a sample bulletin.

Communion is celebrated on first and third Sundays. All are welcome to participate, no questions asked.

On second and fourth Sundays, we hold Sunday School classes for children ages 4-12 during the worship service. On other Sundays, children learn about the faith through participation in worship.

What to expect: We're relatively traditional, but not stuffy.

  • Do have: three hymns (a blend of old and new), a sermon, participation by children.
  • Also have: a hearing loop, large-print bulletins, an accessible ramp.
  • Don't have: a dress code, snobby attitudes, a praise band.
  • Do want: singers (and non-), people who don't mind friendly people. Kids are always welcome, too.
