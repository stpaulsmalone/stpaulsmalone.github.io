Our Pastor

Rev. Daniel Schultz, also known as "Pastor Dan," has ministered to the people of St. Paul's since January 2016. His wife Jennifer teaches psychology at Marian University. Their son William attends Fond du Lac High School.

Dan grew up in Madison as the son and grandson of United Church of Christ pastors. After graduating from the University of Wisconsin with degrees in English and History, he took some time to find himself. Eventually, he joined a UCC congregation in Minneapolis and soon after moved to Atlanta, where he received his Masters of Divinity from Candler School of Theology at Emory University. He was ordained in 1999.

Dan has served mostly small, mostly rural congregations in Georgia, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsin. An accomplished blogger and writer, he has been published in The Christian Century, The Guardian, and Religion Dispatches, among other outlets. He is the author of one book. Dan has occasionally taught as an adjunct instructor at Marian University and Lakeland University in theology and first-year studies. He currently serves as the editor of the Northeast Association newsletter.

While in between congregations, Dan earned a technical diploma in Web Design and Development from Moraine Park Technical College. He enjoys baking bread, photography and bike rides with his family. He can often be found in the company of his
photogenic beagle Taco.
